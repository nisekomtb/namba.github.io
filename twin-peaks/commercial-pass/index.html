Twin Peaks Bike Park: Commercial Pass

At the Niseko Area Mountain Bike Association (NAMBA), we believe in providing an open and accessible experience for all mountain bike enthusiasts. The Twin Peaks Bike Park is a haven for riders, and we're thrilled to offer free access to the public. However, for those seeking to guide or teach within the park as part of a company, we introduce our Commercial Pass program.

Commercial Pass Overview

Exposure

Your brand or business will be recognized as a supporter of NAMBA no matter how much you sponsor or donate.

Improved infrastructure

The infrastructure we will be contributing to will include trails, skills parks, viewing stations, walking trails, running trails, signage, and snowshoe trails.

Tangible assets

Your contribution will go towards something that will continue to be used for years to come, something that will grow, evolve and improve.

Participation

All members will receive information on dig days, ways to participate and contribute.

1. Free Access for All, Commercial Pass for Guides:

  • The Twin Peaks Bike Park remains free for public use.
  • Companies wishing to guide or teach within the park require a Commercial Pass.

2. Ways to Obtain a Commercial Pass:

  • Contribute to the park, and you automatically gain access.
  • Higuma sponsors and trail sponsors receive X commercial passes for their business.

3. Become a Sponsor:

  • If your company is not yet a sponsor but is intrigued, explore more on our Partner Page.
  • Sponsorship is a meaningful way to support NAMBA and gain additional benefits, including Commercial Passes.

4. Commercial Pass Purchase:

  • For companies opting not to sponsor, Commercial Passes are available for purchase.
  • The cost is $$$ per year for X people.

Your Support Matters

Every contribution, whether through sponsorship or purchasing Commercial Passes, directly supports NAMBA in maintaining and enhancing the Twin Peaks Bike Park. Join us in fostering a vibrant mountain biking community in the Niseko area.

What is a commercial pass?

The Twin Peaks Bike Park will always be free to use to members of the public, but if represent a company and want to guide or teach within the park, then you will require a commercial pass to do so.

So how do you obtain a commercial pass?

There a multiple ways to obtain a commercial pass, but simply; if you contribute to the park then you have access.

If your company is a Higuma sponsor, or one of our special sponsors, such as a trail sponosr, then you automatically get X commercial passes for your business.

If your company isnt already a sponsor, but this sounds like something you would be interested in, you can find out more imformation over on our Partner page.

If sponsorhip isnt the best way for your company to support NAMBA, then you can choose to buy commerical passes.

  • Free to use and open to the public *excluding business use
  • 13+ kilometers across 6 distinct trails, plus 9 kilometers planned for 2024
  • Downhill trails with over 200m of descent
  • Skills park to build confidence and improve technique at the mountain base
  • Flow trails, tech trails, downhill trails - trails for all riders and skill levels
  • Located in the heart of the Niseko area

The Twin Peaks Bike Park is a completely free to use public mountain bike park located in the resort region of Niseko, Japan. The park, designed in collaboration with world renowned trail building and design company Allegra Tourismus, is built with everyrider in mind. The Twin Peaks Bike Park is transforming Niseko into a world class trail destination for locals, domestic travelers and international travelers alike.
