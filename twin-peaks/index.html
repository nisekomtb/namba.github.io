Twin Peaks Bike Park

A free community bike park

The Twin Peaks Bike Park in Niseko, Japan is a world-class mountain biking destination designed in collaboration with renowned trail building company Allegra of Switzerland. The bike park offers free access to the public, and trails for all ages and abilities.

Whilst the trails are free to members of the general public, guides, instructors and tour operators must purchase commercial operating passes. For more information on these commercial passes, click here.

Navigating around the bike park

Trail markers:

These signposts are located at all trailheads throughout the Twin Peaks Bike Park. Each signpost provides essential trail information helping you plan a safe and enjoyable ride.

Trail name:

Displays the name of the trail you're out riding

Trail difficulty:

Shows the difficulty level of the trail, rated according to IMBA’s difficulty levels.

Sponsor:

Acknowledges the generous support of the sponsor who supported the building of this trail!

Location code:

An alphanumeric code used for identifying your location, and navigating the park - useful for meeting up with friends, or for informing emergency responders of your location.

Trail direction:

Indicates the direction of the trail

(uphill, downhill, one-way, etc.).

Trail users:

Specifies the permitted trail users

(MTB only, multi-use).

Trail info:

A scannable QR code with detailed information on the trail, interactive maps and much more - powered by Trailforks.

NAMBA:

The nonprofit mountain bike association, bike park manager and advocacy group responsible for managing and maintaining the trail network.
