Article 18 (Use of funds)

The contributor of the crowdfunding campaign entrusts NAMTBA to responsibly allocate funds received through this campaign to the construction of the Twin Peaks trail park phase 1 to be completed in 2022. If NAMTBA receives funds in excess of the required amount to complete phase 1, the excess funds will be used for phase 2 and 3 of the park to be completed in 2023.

The contributor understands the Twin Peaks trail park will be built on Kutchan council land. NAMTBA leases the land from the Kutchan council under the following conditions:

  1. All the trails on Twin Peaks trail park will be constructed on land that is not intended for forestry purposes. However, the land will be leased on an annual basis to give the council the opportunity to forest the area if it chooses to do so. It is understood that the council will continue granting access to the area in perpetuity due to enriching the lives of the community, creating employment, and attracting more visitors to the area.
  2. To abide by National Forestry regulations trails must be no more than 1 meter wide. The contracted trail builder is aware of this restriction and will not prohibit them from building a sustainable, fun to ride trail network.
  3. Do not enter the town-owned forest area other than the permitted area.
  4. Priority must be given to forest maintenance (thinning, clear cutting, planting, and mowing) by the town in order to maintain the function of the forest as a water source irrigation security forest. (Even if there is a course within the construction area, priority will be given to the construction work, and the town will not restore the course to its current condition after construction.)
  5. When constructing the course, consideration shall be given to land conservation, water source recharge, environmental preservation, etc., and measures shall be taken to prevent earth and sand from flowing into the area concerned and surrounding areas, collapsing, and causing other disasters.
  6. Appropriate drainage to be constructed to allow for rainwater and snowmelt water generated by the undulations of the mountains and forests as a result of course development.
  7. The use of fire, such as cigarettes and bonfires, is prohibited in the area.
  8. The applicant shall dispose of fallen trees and other debris in the area.
  9. No vehicles other than bicycles and ebikes are allowed in the use area.
  10. The period of use is from beginning of May to the end of October. In principle it is prohibited to enter during the winter.
  11. Garbage disposal in the area of use is prohibited. In addition regular trash collecting should be practiced.
  12. When there is a bear sighting, the course must be closed and warnings issued. In addition, the applicant shall promptly notify the town of any information regarding the siting of bears.
  13. The use of the course must not be transferred or leased to a second party.
  14. Do not use for any purpose other than the intended purpose.
